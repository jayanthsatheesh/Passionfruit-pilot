The Graveyard of Unused Hobbies

We’ve all done it. Bought a guitar with dreams of weekend jam sessions. A fancy DSLR for “getting into photography.” A PS5 for gaming marathons. But life happens — and soon that expensive gear gathers dust in a corner.

Modern consumers are optimistic but time-starved. They crave new experiences, but underestimate the time or commitment it takes to pursue them. This leads to impulse purchases, followed by guilt, clutter, and wasted money.

There has to be a smarter way to try.

People don’t always want to own — they want to try.

We’re building a platform that lets users rent high-quality hobby gear — musical instruments, gaming consoles, cameras, bikes, drones and more — for short bursts of exploration. Think of it as a test-drive for hobbies.

No long-term cost. No clutter. No regret.

Why Now?

Access Over Ownership

Urban millennials and Gen Z increasingly prefer access to experiences over owning material possessions.

The Rise of Minimalism

A desire for environmental consciousness and decluttering is changing consumer habits.

Resale is a Hassle

Selling used goods on informal platforms lacks the convenience and quality assurance users now expect.

An Unclaimed Category

The Indian hobby rental market is fragmented, with no single brand owning the space with a reputation built on trust.
